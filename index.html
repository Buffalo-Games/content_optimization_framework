Your two tools

The Reference Tool

This page. It guides your inputs, validates your content against field specs, and surfaces relevant keywords. It never writes copy — that’s the AI’s job.

Your AI Assistant

Claude or ChatGPT. It takes your structured inputs and generates polished, on-brand product listings. Always use the system prompt below to prime it first.

The rule: Reference Tool first → AI second. Always.

8-step workflow

1
Open this tool alongside your AI session.
Keep this tab pinned. Run your AI in a separate tab or window. You’ll move between them at several points in the workflow.
2
Run the Intake Wizard.
Select New Item, Extension/Variant, or Digital. Follow the guided steps to ensure you have all required inputs before touching the AI.
3
Validate your fields in the Field Validator tab.
Aim for a green readiness score before proceeding. Fix any fields flagged red — they’ll cause issues in Syndigo.
4
Check the Term Bank.
Filter to your market and copy relevant terms. These go into your keyword field in the prompt template — don’t skip this step.
5
Open your AI session and paste the system prompt.
The system prompt below must be pasted at the start of every new AI session. It sets the context the AI needs to apply the full 12-dimension framework.
6
Paste your completed inputs using a prompt template.
Choose Option 1 (Guided Foundation), Option 2 (Image-Led Quick Build), or Option 3 (Keyword-First) based on what you have available.
7
Validate AI output in the Field Validator.
Paste the AI’s title, bullets, description, and keywords into the validator. Fix anything flagged before moving forward.
8
Enter into Syndigo.
Enter title, bullets, description, keywords, AND attribute fields. Syndigo is the source of truth — this is where all content originates and syndicates outward.

New Item — 6 steps

1Gather product basics

You need: full product name, UPC/ASIN, brand, materials, dimensions, weight, and piece count or component list. Pull from internal specs or packaging before opening the AI.

2Define your market

Assign to one primary market: Puzzles, Preschool, Activities, Games, or Rec Sports. This drives keyword selection and tone. When in doubt, choose the market matching the primary buyer persona.

3Run keyword research

Use Helium10, ViralLaunch, or JungleScout. Pull 8–10 terms ranked by monthly search volume. Also check the Term Bank tab for category-specific seed terms. You need these before filling the prompt template.

4Write your Core Product Summary

One sentence: what it is, who it’s for, and what makes it unique. Example: “A 1000-piece nature jigsaw puzzle designed for adults seeking a mindful, screen-free hobby.” This becomes dimension 1 in the AI prompt.

5Identify 3 key differentiators

What does this product do better or differently than the two closest competitors? These become your bullet point anchors. Check competitor listings on Amazon for gaps you can fill.

6Fill Option 1 prompt and run

Go to the How to Use tab, copy the system prompt (paste into AI first), then copy Option 1 — Guided Foundation and fill in your gathered data. Validate output in Field Validator before entering Syndigo.

Ready? Go to the How to Use tab and copy the system prompt + Option 1 template.

Digital Product

Dimensions that change

Dimensions 8 (Care & Storage) and 5 (How to Use/Play) require reframing for digital. Storage becomes “Download & access” guidance. Care instructions become system/device requirements. Dimension 3 (Features) should lead with format benefits: instant delivery, no physical storage required, always available.

Syndigo adjustments

Set product type to Digital in Syndigo. Weight and dimension fields should reflect packaging if a physical component exists; otherwise mark as N/A. Check with Dan or Leo on which attribute fields apply to digital SKUs — schema differs by category.

Platform compliance

Amazon restricts certain digital product categories. Confirm the product is eligible for the target category before generating content. Ensure all download/access instructions are accurate — Amazon removes listings with incorrect digital product claims. EastPoint digital products may require additional compliance review.

Amazon-first content workflow

How content moves from creation to live Amazon pages — and back.

1
Syndigo — source of truth
All content originates here. Every workflow starts in Syndigo. Title, bullets, long description, backend keywords, and all attribute fields are entered and maintained in Syndigo — never in Amazon Vendor Central directly.
2
Syndigo publishes to Amazon
Syndigo syndicates your content directly to Amazon’s product catalog. Data goes live: title, bullets, attributes, and A+ content modules. The sync is automated — any manual change in Vendor Central will be overwritten on the next push.
3
Autopilot.ai enriches live Amazon data
Autopilot.ai reads live Amazon listing data and applies optimization: keyword signals, competitive analysis, and GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) tuning. It works on top of what Syndigo published.
4
Autopilot publishes optimized content to Amazon
Autopilot’s enhanced content goes live on Amazon. Stronger keyword coverage, better GEO signals, more competitive positioning. The listing is now optimized beyond what Syndigo alone could produce.
5
Autopilot injects data back into Syndigo Amazon loop closes here
Critical step — Autopilot pushes its optimized content back into Syndigo as the updated source of truth. This prevents content drift: Syndigo remains current with whatever Autopilot published. If this step breaks, Syndigo and Amazon fall out of sync.
6
Retailer-specific adaptation Future State
Walmart, Target, and other retail partners each require adapted content — different title lengths, attribute schemas, and image specifications. This is not a simple re-syndication from Amazon. It’s a purposefully separate workflow currently in planning.
Why not auto-syndicate to Walmart? Walmart titles are 50–75 characters vs. Amazon’s ~200. Sparky’s attribute schema differs from Rufus. Target requires different image specs and creative guidelines. Each retailer needs purpose-built content — a copy-paste from Amazon creates a worse listing, not a faster one.
The golden rule: Always start in Syndigo. Changes made in Amazon Vendor Central or Walmart Supplier Center will be overwritten on the next sync. Syndigo is always the source of truth.
